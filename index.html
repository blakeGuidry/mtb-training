Cadence Targets

Default to 85–95 RPM for general training. Train both ends of the spectrum for BCBR singletrack.

SessionRPMWhy
Z2 Endurance (default)85–95Best power/efficiency balance. All-day cadence.
Sweet Spot / Threshold85–95Let ERG control resistance. Smooth pedal strokes.
VO2 Max intervals90–100Higher cadence reduces muscular fatigue, more intervals before failure.
Low-cadence torque50–65High-torque for steep loose climbs.
Spin-up drills100–12030s acceleration in easy gear. Grind-to-spin transition.
Microbursts~95–105Let cadence rise naturally during surges.
Race day50–110+Terrain dictates. Full spectrum readiness.
ERG Mode Guide

ERG ON: Structured intervals — threshold, VO2 max, sweet spot, microbursts.

ERG OFF: Free rides, recovery, climbing routes, low-cadence torque work.
